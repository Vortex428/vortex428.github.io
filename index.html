PROJECT / 01

Arduino Combat Flight
Instrument Panel

Arduino Mega C++ Processing MPU-6050 IMU GY-521 Serial / I²C

Objective: Build a physical flight instrument panel that reads real motion data and renders a live 3D cockpit simulation.

Starting from scratch with an Elegoo Mega Starter Kit and a GY-521 IMU module, I designed a complete hardware-software pipeline: the Arduino reads pitch, roll, and yaw data from the MPU-6050 over I²C, packages it via Serial, and streams it to a companion application built in Processing.

  • Wired the GY-521 IMU to the Mega over I²C; tuned the DMP for stable angle output
  • Wrote non-blocking Arduino firmware using millis()-based timing loops
  • Built a 3D cockpit visualizer in Processing — then extended it to a full combat sim
  • Implemented procedural terrain generation, enemy AI, and weapon mechanics
  • Debugged serial communication handshake between Arduino (COM6) and PC
Result Fully playable first-person flight simulation driven entirely by physical tilt of the hardware panel. Pitch, roll, and yaw map 1:1 to cockpit orientation in real time.

PROJECT / 02

PIR-Triggered UVC
Light Safety Controller

Arduino C++ PIR Sensor Relay Module Safety Logic millis() Timing

Objective: Automate a UVC germicidal lamp so it only activates when a room is unoccupied — for safe, hands-free disinfection.

UVC light is effective for disinfection but dangerous to human skin and eyes. I designed a controller that uses a passive infrared (PIR) motion sensor to detect occupancy and drives a relay to cut power to the UVC lamp the moment presence is detected.

  • Architected occupancy-state machine: IDLE → WARMING → ACTIVE → COOLDOWN
  • Replaced all delay() calls with millis() for fully non-blocking behavior
  • Debugged inverted PIR logic and missing else branch in activation flow
  • Resolved CLI Daemon Offline board error and compiler semicolon fault
  • Tested relay switching with inductive load protection in mind
Result Reliable, interrupt-safe controller that immediately kills UVC output on motion detection. Zero occupancy exposure risk during a timed disinfection cycle.

PROJECT / 03

Hoverboard Motor
Electric Go-Kart

BLDC Motors 600V Wiring Custom Controller Throttle Control Mechanical Fab Frame Assembly

Objective: Salvage hoverboard hub motors and engineer them into a rideable electric go-kart with a custom speed controller and safe high-current wiring.

Starting from salvaged hoverboard BLDC hub motors, I designed and assembled a full electric go-kart from the ground up. The build involved mechanical fabrication, custom electronics, and a full wiring overhaul — ultimately pushing the kart to its maximum rated speed.

  • Repurposed hoverboard hub motors as drive wheels, adapting mounting to a custom steel frame
  • Designed and wired a custom throttle control circuit for variable speed drive
  • Drilled and bolted the frame, seat mounts, and wheel assemblies — full mechanical assembly
  • Managed routing of high-current wiring for motor controllers and power distribution
  • Upgraded entire wiring harness to 600V-rated cable to eliminate resistance and unlock top speed
  • Tested incrementally at each phase — slow crawl → controlled run → full throttle
Result Fully operational electric go-kart capable of reaching maximum rated motor speed. The 600V wire upgrade eliminated thermal throttling and allowed safe full-power operation.

PROJECT / 04

MESA Aerospace
Cargo Glider

1st — NorCal Regionals Aerospace Eng. Aerodynamics Load Optimization Structural Design Team Competition

Objective: Design, build, and fly a cargo glider that maximizes payload delivery accuracy and efficiency — competing at the MESA NorCal Regional Championship.

As part of my school's MESA (Mathematics, Engineering, Science Achievement) team, we entered the Aerospace Engineering division and competed in the NorCal Regionals.   • Designed the cargo bay for secure payload retention throughout the flight arc
  • Iterated on glider balance by adjusting CG position relative to center of lift
  • Conducted test flights and refined control surfaces for stable, predictable glide
  • Collaborated with teammates on structural integrity vs. weight tradeoffs
  • Presented design rationale and engineering decisions to judges at competition
Result — 1st Place, NorCal Regionals Flawless performance at competition. Our glider outperformed all regional competitors in cargo delivery. Advancing as NorCal champions — a direct outcome of disciplined engineering and design iteration.
